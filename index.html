  • Group Processes

    Assignment 2 posed several challenges to our group in the opening week or two, which left us playing catch-up for the remainder of the project. The first of these challenges was that the assignment started over the holiday period, and two of our original six members were away while the rest of the group started introducing themselves and early tasks were delegated. While this was a relatively unavoidable challenge, it meant that the opening two weeks of our group effort felt slightly disjointed and inefficient while everyone got on the same page. This issue was compounded when two members of our group left abruptly and without notice. These two team-members had taken on a number of small tasks, which meant that the group had to pick up additional tasks at short notice in order to complete the assignment.

    Despite these challenges – or perhaps because of them – our group functioned well throughout the vast majority of the project. Feedback from across the team showed that meetings were relatively productive, work was delegated in a fair manner, and all individuals appeared to be willing to go above and beyond to help their colleagues complete tasks. However, that is not to say that there isn’t significant room for improvement in the way the group operates as we work through this assignment. More specifically, our team will look to improve on the following areas: meeting scheduling, meeting efficiency, regular feedback, time-management.

    Meeting Scheduling

    One area that our team will look to improve upon for the remainder of our project regards the scheduling of meetings. During our work on Assignment 2, our team endeavoured to hold regular meetings at least once per week. While this generally allowed our group to efficiently delegate tasks and provide progress updates on our work, we found that it was rare that all-four members were present for meetings. With this in mind, our group will look schedule meetings further in advance which will allow team members more time to ensure that they can fit meetings into their calendars.

    Likewise, we will look to increase the number of meetings to at least two per week. This will allow individual team members to provide more regular updates on their progress with tasks and will aid with some of the improvements in time management which will be outlined below.

    Meeting Efficiency

    While it is important that we meet more regularly, it is equally important that these meetings are run as efficiently as possible. One of the major pieces of feedback from within the group regarding the meetings held during Assignment 2 was that meetings occasionally lacked structure. This is likely due to the fact that we worked from a single ‘live’ document to delegate tasks to individuals and maintain accountability over deadlines. While this document served us well at the time, it has been acknowledged that we failed to write formal agendas or take extensive minutes during meetings, both of which would have likely improved the efficiency of team meetings. This is something we will be looking to rectify during Assignment 3, not only in an effort to improve the efficiency of team meetings themselves but would also provide team members who are unable to attend meetings for any reason another avenue to catch up on conversations and ensure that all team members remain on the same page.

    Regular Feedback

    One additional point of improvement that the team will look to make in Assignment 3 regards team members providing each other with feedback on work as tasks are completed. More specifically, the team will look to review each other’s work as it is uploaded to SharePoint and provide constructive feedback where we feel work could be improved. This will hopefully lead to an overall improve in both the quality and the cohesiveness of our finished product.

    Time-Management

    While our time-management throughout Assignment 2 was generally good throughout the project, we did find ourselves making significant, last-minute changes in the hours leading up to the submission deadline. This meant that we did not have adequate time to thoroughly proof-read our final product and suggest improvements where necessary. Our team expects that some of the issues revolving around time-management will be largely dealt with by adhering to our proposed improvements in meeting scheduling and efficiency, as well as by encouraging team-members to provide regular feedback on work as mentioned above. Likewise, we will set deadlines for smaller tasks that will allow us to spend the final week adjusting our final submission, rather than writing up core content at the last minute.

    Career Plans

    Considering the fact that the Information Technology industry is home to a huge number of varied career paths, it is not surprising that all four members of our group have distinctly unique ideal jobs and career plans. For example, Nelson Lay has ambitions of becoming a Cloud Solutions Architect which would see him work with clients to design and implement tailor-made cloud-based solutions. In contrast, Joe Sharratt is hoping to enter into the security and intelligence sector as a Team Leader for the Australian Federal Police. This role would see him apply I.T. skills in a slightly more abstract way through complex database management and through the designing of bespoke software tools. Meanwhile, Victoria Sabino is striving to enter the world of mobile gaming as a Senior Software Engineer for Riot Games. Here she hopes to specialise in cybersecurity and develop anti-cheat tools. Finally, Jordan Glen is attempting to enter the finance sector as an Algorithmic Trader where he will use his I.T. knowledge to build and manipulate computer systems designed to give him and his clients an edge when stock-trading.

    Interestingly, despite the variation in career goals between all four members of our team, all of our career plans centre around formal, tertiary education. In fact, three out of four team members have stated that they will look to complete a degree in IT, with the remaining team member – Joe Sharratt – aiming to complete a degree in Security Studies. In addition, all four team members have acknowledged a desire to pursue further education after completing their core degree programs. These additional courses are generally more niche and tailored to the specific sectors that each individual is hoping to get into. Jordan Glen’s career plan serves as a great illustration of this, as he is looking to complete courses in financial programming and quantitative analysis once his studies at RMIT are finished. Finally, some team members such as Victoria Sabino have acknowledged that it is unlikely that they will be able to compete for their ideal job straight after graduating from university and have included taking on more junior or entry-level roles as a way to gain industry experience before working their way up.
